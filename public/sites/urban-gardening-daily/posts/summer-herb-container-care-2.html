Summer Herb Survival: Expert Tips for Container Gardeners

Keep herbs thriving in summer

Takeaway

Proper container care is crucial for summer herb survival

What happened

As summer approaches, many apartment dwellers and balcony gardeners struggle to keep their herbs alive. High temperatures and intense sunlight can be detrimental to these plants. With proper care, however, herbs can continue to thrive in containers throughout the summer months.

Why it matters

Herbs are a great addition to any urban garden, providing fresh flavor to a variety of dishes. By keeping them healthy and productive, gardeners can enjoy a continuous harvest and make the most of their outdoor space. This is especially important for apartment dwellers with limited space, as herbs can be grown in small containers on balconies or windowsills.

How to think about it

To keep herbs healthy in summer, gardeners should focus on providing adequate moisture, fertilization, and pruning. Containers should be checked daily for water, and herbs should be fertilized regularly to promote growth. Pruning can help control the size and shape of the plants, while also encouraging new growth and preventing the plants from becoming leggy.

Pros
  • Improved flavor
  • Increased yield
  • Space-saving
Cons
  • Requires daily maintenance
  • Susceptible to pests
  • Limited root depth
Watch out

Be cautious of overheating, which can quickly kill herb plants in containers

FAQ

How often should I water my herbs in summer?

Check the soil daily, and water when the top inch feels dry

What type of fertilizer is best for herbs?

A balanced, water-soluble fertilizer is ideal for herbs

Can I grow herbs in partial shade?

While some herbs can tolerate partial shade, most require full sun to produce well
