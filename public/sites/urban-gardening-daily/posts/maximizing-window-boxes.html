Squeeze More From Your Window Boxes

Grow more with less space

Takeaway

Choosing the right plants can significantly increase yield in small window boxes

What happened

Window boxes are a staple for urban gardeners, providing a compact way to grow herbs, flowers, and vegetables. However, their small size can limit growth. Recent experiments with layering plants and utilizing vertical space have shown promising results. By maximizing space, urban gardeners can enjoy a wider variety of plants

Why it matters

For apartment dwellers and balcony gardeners, space is at a premium. Being able to maximize window boxes means more opportunities to grow a diverse range of plants, from herbs for cooking to flowers for aesthetic appeal. This not only enhances the gardening experience but also increases the potential for a bountiful harvest

How to think about it

When planning your window box, consider the mature size of the plants, their growth habits, and how they can be layered or trained to use the available space efficiently. Selecting plants that have similar growing requirements can also simplify care. Additionally, incorporating a trellis or a self-watering system can help optimize growth and reduce maintenance

Pros
  • Increased yield
  • Improved aesthetics
  • Space efficiency
Cons
  • Higher maintenance
  • Initial investment
  • Limited plant selection
Watch out

Be cautious of overwatering, as window boxes can be prone to waterlogged soil due to their small size and potentially limited drainage

FAQ

What plants are best for window boxes?

Choose compact or trailing varieties of herbs, flowers, or vegetables

How often should I water my window box?

Water when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch, avoiding overwatering

Can I use regular potting soil in my window box?

It's recommended to use a well-draining potting mix specifically designed for containers to prevent waterlogging
