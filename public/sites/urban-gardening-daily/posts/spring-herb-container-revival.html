Breathing New Life into Last Year's Herb Containers

Revive old herb containers for spring

Takeaway

Don't discard last year's herb containers, revive them for a fresh spring harvest

What happened

As winter comes to a close, many of us are left with old, worn-out herb containers that seem beyond repair. However, with a little creativity and some practical know-how, these containers can be restored to their former glory. By giving them a thorough clean and replenishing the soil, you can breathe new life into your herb garden

Why it matters

Reviving old containers not only reduces waste but also saves you money on purchasing new ones. Moreover, it allows you to maintain the health and continuity of your herb garden, ensuring a consistent supply of fresh herbs throughout the year. This approach also helps to minimize the environmental impact of your gardening practices

How to think about it

To revive your old herb containers, start by thoroughly cleaning them with soap and water, then replace the old soil with a fresh, well-draining mix. Next, prune back any dead or leggy herbs, and finally, add some new herb seedlings to fill any gaps. By following these simple steps, you can create a thriving and vibrant herb garden in no time

Pros
  • Reduces waste
  • Saves money
  • Maintains garden health
Cons
  • Time-consuming
  • Requires some effort
  • May not always be possible
Watch out

Be cautious not to transfer any diseases from the old soil to the new, by properly disinfecting the containers before reuse

FAQ

What is the best way to clean old herb containers?

Mix 1 part bleach with 10 parts water and soak the containers for 10-15 minutes before rinsing thoroughly

Can I reuse the old soil in my herb containers?

No, it's recommended to replace the old soil with a fresh, well-draining mix to prevent the transfer of diseases and pests

How often should I prune my herbs to keep them healthy?

Prune your herbs regularly, ideally every 1-2 weeks, to encourage bushy growth and prevent them from becoming leggy
