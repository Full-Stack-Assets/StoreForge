Growing Microgreens on Your Windowsill

Boost flavor with minimal space

Takeaway

Microgreens can be harvested in as little as 7-10 days with proper care and conditions

What happened

Microgreens have gained popularity for their ease of growth and nutrient-dense profiles. These young, immature greens can be grown from a variety of herb and vegetable seeds. With the right conditions, anyone can start growing microgreens, even in small spaces like apartment windowsills.

Why it matters

Microgreens offer a way for apartment dwellers to have a constant supply of fresh, healthy greens year-round, regardless of the season. They require minimal space and care, making them perfect for urban gardening. By incorporating microgreens into their diet, individuals can add variety and nutrition to their meals.

How to think about it

To start growing microgreens, consider the type of seeds to use, the amount of light they require, and the watering schedule. Most microgreens need about 4-6 hours of direct sunlight or 12-14 hours under grow lights. Water them gently but thoroughly, and harvest when the leaves are 1-3 inches tall. Regular harvests will encourage new growth.

Pros
  • Space-efficient
  • Quick to harvest
  • Nutrient-rich
Cons
  • Requires consistent watering
  • Can be prone to mold
  • Limited crop selection
Watch out

Be cautious of overwatering, which can lead to mold and poor germination rates

FAQ

What seeds are best for microgreens?

Lettuce, kale, and radish seeds are popular choices

How often should I water microgreens?

Water them when the soil feels dry to the touch, usually once a day

Can I grow microgreens indoors without sunlight?

Yes, using grow lights can provide the necessary light for healthy growth
