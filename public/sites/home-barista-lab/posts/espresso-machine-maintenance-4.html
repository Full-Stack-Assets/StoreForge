The Daily Grind: How Regular Cleaning Saves Your Espresso

Keep your machine in top shape

Takeaway

Regular cleaning extends the life of your espresso machine and improves flavor

What happened

A recent survey of home baristas found that many espresso machine owners neglect regular cleaning, leading to decreased performance and flavor. This can result in a buildup of old coffee oils and residue, affecting the taste of every shot. By incorporating a daily cleaning routine, home baristas can prevent this issue

Why it matters

A well-maintained espresso machine is essential for producing high-quality shots, and neglecting cleaning can lead to expensive repairs or even replacement. Regular cleaning also helps to prevent bacterial growth and keeps the machine in good working order. This, in turn, ensures that every cup of coffee tastes its best

How to think about it

To keep your espresso machine in top condition, set aside a few minutes each day to wipe down the exterior, clean the brew group, and descale the machine regularly. This simple routine can make a significant difference in the flavor and longevity of your machine. Start by incorporating one new cleaning habit into your daily routine and build from there

Pros
  • Improved flavor
  • Extended machine life
  • Easy to implement
Cons
  • Time-consuming
  • Requires effort
  • Additional cost for cleaning supplies
Watch out

Be careful not to use abrasive cleaners, as they can damage the machine's surfaces

FAQ

How often should I clean my espresso machine?

Daily, with a deeper clean once a week

What cleaning products should I use?

Mild detergents and water, or specialized espresso machine cleaning solutions

Can I use a mixture of water and vinegar to clean my machine?

Yes, a 1:1 mixture of water and vinegar can be used to descale and clean the machine
