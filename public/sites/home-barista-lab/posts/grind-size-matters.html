The Grind Size Experiment: Boosting Flavor in Home Brews

Perfecting grind size boosts flavor

Takeaway

Adjusting grind size can significantly improve the taste of your home-brewed coffee

What happened

In a recent experiment, we tested the impact of different grind sizes on the flavor of pour-over coffee. The results showed that even small adjustments can make a significant difference. From over-extracted and bitter to perfectly balanced, grind size proved to be a crucial factor.

Why it matters

Getting the grind size right is essential because it affects the rate of extraction, which in turn impacts the flavor. Incorrect grind sizes can lead to under or over-extraction, resulting in subpar taste. Understanding how to adjust grind size gives home baristas more control over their brewing process.

How to think about it

When adjusting grind size, consider the type of coffee bean, the brewing method, and personal taste preferences. A burr grinder is the best tool for achieving consistent grind sizes. Start with a medium grind and adjust based on the flavor profile you're aiming for.

Pros
  • Improved flavor
  • Increased control
  • Easy to adjust
Cons
  • Requires a burr grinder
  • Trial and error
  • Sensitive to change
Watch out

Be cautious not to over-adjust, as this can lead to inconsistent results and frustration

FAQ

What is the ideal grind size for pour-over?

A medium to fine grind is usually recommended

Can I use a blade grinder for adjusting grind size?

No, blade grinders are not recommended as they can generate heat and inconsistently grind the beans

How often should I clean my burr grinder?

Regular cleaning is essential; aim to clean your grinder at least once a week to prevent old coffee oils from affecting the taste
