The grinder tune-up: how a simple clean can transform your brew

Get more from your beans with a clean grinder

Takeaway

Regular grinder maintenance can significantly improve the flavor and consistency of your coffee

What happened

For many home baristas, the coffee grinder is a workhorse that's often overlooked until it breaks down. A recent survey found that over 70% of enthusiasts never clean their grinders, leading to a buildup of old coffee oils and residue. This can result in stale or bitter flavors and uneven extraction.

Why it matters

A clean grinder is essential for achieving optimal flavor and aroma in your coffee. Old coffee oils and residue can impart unwanted flavors to your brew, while a clogged grinder can lead to channeling and uneven extraction. By cleaning your grinder regularly, you can ensure that every shot is the best it can be.

How to think about it

To keep your grinder in top condition, make cleaning a regular part of your routine. Start by brushing out any loose coffee grounds, then wipe down the burrs and grinding chamber with a soft cloth. For a deeper clean, mix equal parts water and white vinegar in the grinder and run it for a few seconds to loosen any tough residue.

Pros
  • Improved flavor
  • Increased consistency
  • Extended grinder lifespan
Cons
  • Time-consuming
  • Requires regular maintenance
  • May require replacement parts
Watch out

Be careful not to get any liquids into the grinder's electrical components, as this can cause damage or even a short circuit

FAQ

How often should I clean my grinder?

It's recommended to clean your grinder at least once a week, or more often if you use it daily

Can I use a dishwasher to clean my grinder?

No, it's not recommended to put your grinder in the dishwasher, as the high heat and harsh detergents can damage the burrs and other components

What's the best way to store my grinder when not in use?

Store your grinder in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture
