Bring Back the Beige

Revive your IBM PS/2

Takeaway

Restoration is key to preserving retro computing history

What happened

The IBM PS/2 line was introduced in the late 1980s, featuring a range of models from desktops to laptops. Many of these machines still exist today, albeit in various states of disrepair. Restoration experts have been working to revive these systems, often using original parts and techniques. As a result, many restored IBM PS/2s are now available for enthusiasts to purchase and enjoy.

Why it matters

Restoring vintage computers like the IBM PS/2 helps preserve the history of the tech industry and provides a unique window into the development of modern computing. By preserving these systems, we can gain a deeper understanding of how technology has evolved over time. Furthermore, restored vintage computers can serve as a valuable educational tool, allowing students to learn about the inner workings of early computer systems.

How to think about it

When restoring a vintage computer, it's essential to approach the project with a clear plan and a thorough understanding of the system's components. Start by researching the specific model and its known issues, then gather the necessary tools and replacement parts. Be prepared to troubleshoot and adapt, as vintage systems can be unpredictable. Consider joining online forums or communities to connect with other restoration enthusiasts and learn from their experiences.

FAQ

What is the best way to clean a vintage computer?

Use compressed air and a soft-bristled brush to gently remove dust and debris

How do I troubleshoot a faulty vintage computer?

Start by checking the power supply and verifying that all components are properly seated

Where can I find replacement parts for my vintage computer?

Check online marketplaces, specialty retailers, and vintage computer enthusiast communities
