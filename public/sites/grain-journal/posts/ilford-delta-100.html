Shooting with Ilford Delta 100

Get the best from this iconic film stock.

Takeaway

Ilford Delta 100 is ideal for capturing fine details in a variety of lighting conditions.

What happened

Ilford Delta 100 has been a staple in the film photography community for decades, known for its high contrast and fine grain. It's a popular choice among portrait and landscape photographers. Recently, its popularity has surged due to its versatility.

Why it matters

Understanding how to work with Ilford Delta 100 can elevate your photography, allowing for more nuanced and detailed captures. Its ability to handle a wide range of lighting conditions makes it a valuable asset in any photographer's arsenal. By mastering Delta 100, photographers can achieve professional-grade results.

How to think about it

To get the most out of Ilford Delta 100, consider the lighting conditions of your shoot and adjust your exposure accordingly. Practice developing the film to understand its unique characteristics and how they can enhance your photographs. Experiment with different development times to find what works best for your style.

Pros
  • High contrast
  • Fine grain
  • Versatile
Cons
  • Sensitive to overexposure
  • Requires precise development
  • Can be expensive
Watch out

Be cautious of overexposing Ilford Delta 100, as it can lead to lost details in highlights.

FAQ

What is the best developer for Ilford Delta 100?

Ilford DD-X is highly recommended for developing Delta 100.

Can I push Ilford Delta 100?

Yes, Delta 100 can be pushed up to 2 stops with minimal loss in quality.

Is Ilford Delta 100 suitable for beginners?

While it can be used by beginners, its sensitivity and development requirements might make it more challenging for those new to film photography.
