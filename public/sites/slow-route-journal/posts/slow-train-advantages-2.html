Why Slow Trains Beat Flights for Low-Impact Travel

Ditch the plane for a more relaxed ride

Takeaway

Slow trains offer a unique, low-carbon way to see the countryside while traveling

What happened

I recently took a slow train from Paris to Rome, watching the countryside roll by as I chatted with fellow travelers. The journey was relaxing and enjoyable, with plenty of time to read, write, and enjoy the scenery. Along the way, I met several like-minded travelers who shared tips and recommendations for low-impact exploration

Why it matters

As travelers become increasingly aware of their environmental impact, slow trains offer a guilt-free alternative to flying. By choosing the train, travelers can reduce their carbon footprint while supporting local economies and experiencing the unique culture of each region. This approach also fosters a sense of community and connection among travelers

How to think about it

When planning your next trip, consider the slow train as a viable option. Research routes and schedules in advance, and book early to secure the best prices. Don't be afraid to strike up conversations with fellow travelers – you never know who you might meet or what tips you might learn

Pros
  • Relaxed pace
  • Scenic views
  • Low carbon footprint
Cons
  • Longer travel time
  • Limited routes
  • Variable comfort
Watch out

Be sure to research any necessary visas or travel documents before embarking on your slow train journey

FAQ

What is the average speed of a slow train?

Typically around 50-70 km/h

Can I bring my bike on a slow train?

Yes, many slow trains have bike storage or allow bicycles in designated areas

Are slow trains more expensive than flights?

Not always – prices vary depending on the route and time of year, but slow trains can often be a cost-effective option
